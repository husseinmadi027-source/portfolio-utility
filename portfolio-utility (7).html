About Me

I understand electrical distribution systems from the inside out — I spent 2.5 years working in one every day.

I graduated Cum Laude in May 2026 with a BASc in Mechanical and Biomedical Engineering from the University of Ottawa, with a strong foundation in structural mechanics, materials science, thermodynamics, and engineering design. I completed three progressive roles at Hydro Ottawa Limited, building hands-on experience across distribution standards compliance, maintenance and reliability engineering, and GIS and records management in a regulated Ontario utility environment.

Distribution engineering exposure: arc flash analysis using CYMTCC, structural pole modelling in SpidaCalc to CSA C22.1 and OESC, transformer condition assessment via Duval Pentagon dissolved gas analysis, power quality analysis with PiSlicer and PQWeb, infrastructure mapping in GeoMedia, outage root cause investigations, third-party submission compliance review, and Power BI/Tableau reliability dashboards.

I am bilingual in English and French, fluent in Arabic, and conversational in Spanish. I bring both the technical depth of an engineering graduate and the practical perspective of someone who has worked directly in the distribution environment they are designing for.
